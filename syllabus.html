Syllabus for “Principles of Automatic Control”

1. Introduction to Control Systems

  • Understanding Control Systems and their importance in modern technology.
  • Basic terminologies and concepts in control systems.
  • Distinction between Open-Loop and Closed-Loop control systems.
  • Exploring the structure and challenges of Feedback Control Systems.
  • Design approaches for Feedback Control Systems.
  • Historical context and evolution of control systems.

2. Basics of Feedback Control

  • Real-world examples of control systems: Bathroom Toilet Tank, Automobile Driving System, Hydraulic Power Steering Mechanism, Residential Heating System.
  • Introduction to Multi-Input Multi-Output (MIMO) systems.
  • Analysis of complex control systems and their components.
  • Block Diagram of Basic Feedback Structure.

3. Dynamic Systems

  • Exploration of dynamic systems in control engineering.
  • Mathematical modeling and analysis of dynamic systems.

4. Dynamic Response

  • Understanding the response of control systems to dynamic changes.
  • Techniques for evaluating and optimizing system responses.

5. Inverse Laplace Transform

  • Application of inverse Laplace transform in control systems.
  • Methods like partial fraction decomposition for complex functions.

6. Modeling Dynamic Systems

  • Techniques for modeling and simulation of dynamic control systems.

7. Control System Components

  • Comprehensive overview of different components in a control system.
  • Detailed analysis of actuators, controllers, sensors, and their functions.

8. Models of Control Devices and Systems

  • In-depth study of various models used in control devices and systems.
  • Case studies and practical applications.

9. Hardware and Case Studies

  • Exploration of hardware used in control systems.
  • Analysis of real-world case studies for practical understanding.

10. A First Complete Application

  • Applying theoretical knowledge to a complete control system application.

11. Principles of Feedback Control

  • Detailed study of feedback control principles and their applications.

12. Feedback Systems and their Effects

  • Analyzing the effects of feedback mechanisms in control systems.

13. Introduction to Control Systems

  • Revisiting the fundamental concepts in control systems.

14. Stability

  • Understanding the concept of stability in control systems.

15. Stability and Routh Criterion

  • Detailed study of the Routh criterion and its application in assessing stability.

16. Performance of Feedback Systems

  • Evaluating and enhancing the performance of feedback control systems.

17. Design of Feedback Control

  • Strategies and methodologies for designing effective feedback control systems.

18. Advanced Topics in Design of Feedback Control

  • Delving into more complex aspects of feedback control system design.
